A Website is a Shifting House
“How could a website complement what you already do rather than competing or repeating? How can you make it fun or thought-provoking or (insert desired feeling here) for you? How can the process of making and cultivating a website contribute to your approach?”

A website could complement what I’m already doing without competing or repeating by adapting what i’m doing to the web. Something that lives in “the real world” doesn’t have the same needs as something living in the web.

Desired Feeling(s): Productive, joyous, excited.

How to achieve desired feelings: firstly by learning and understanding web, and secondly by adapting to web while still maintaining what i already enjoy (physicality) about my work.

The tedium of making a website is very similar to my existing process.

9.25.23

A Friend Is a Writing

"every line can be revised until it is sent" "a truly instant messenger would display each and every keystroke in real time, instead we have an exchange that sits halfway between speaking and writing"

I've actually thought a lot about this idea of revision when texting. I remember in high school, I felt like I needed space in between each response in any conversation, text or not. I just needed a minute to think and decide what the exact best possible response was. I felt the semi-immediacy of a back-and-forth text conversation gave me just enough time for revisions, and allowed me to be the wittiest version of myself possible. There was no need to think back on what I should have said in a certain moment, because I was able to take that extra few moments and make those extra few revisions.

"the online economy is one of attention"

There's an expected immediacy to everything online, even I notice myself growing impatient when something is buffering too long or someone takes too long to type out their response to me.

10.17.23

The Poetry of Tools

I've decided to focus on Alexei Shulgin's Form Art from 1997.

I feel like it would be extremely difficult to experience this piece in the same way more than once. This is why I was drawn to it, it has an exploratory aspect as well as an expanseiveness. One would have to be very deliberate if they wanted to experience every single little button or pop-up. But what's great about this website is that it's not about each little button or pop-up, but this game-like web object that they all make up together.
